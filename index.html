Raise awareness. Demand accountability. Push for reform.

Break the Silence is a student-built digital activism project addressing police brutality and systemic racism in the United States. The project uses technology to amplify marginalized voices, educate the public, and encourage ethical civic engagement.

Learn

Recent cases in 2024–2025 show that public visibility often determines whether investigations remain active. Digital platforms preserve evidence, amplify community voices, and sustain accountability when traditional media attention fades.

  • Systemic racism: Policies and institutional practices that consistently produce unequal outcomes across racial groups.
  • Accountability: Civilian review boards, body-camera transparency, limits on qualified immunity, and de-escalation standards.
  • Ethical advocacy: Focusing on verified information, avoiding harassment, and prioritizing community safety.

Take Action

This campaign encourages ethical digital activism by sharing verified information, contacting representatives, supporting community organizations, and promoting policy-based reform rather than personal attacks.
