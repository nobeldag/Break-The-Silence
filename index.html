Raise awareness. Demand accountability. Push for reform.

Break the Silence is a digital activism campaign focused on exposing police brutality and systemic racism in the United States. The project uses technology and social media to amplify marginalized voices, educate the public, and promote ethical civic engagement.

Learn

Systemic Racism

Systemic racism refers to institutional policies and practices that consistently produce unequal outcomes across racial groups, even without explicit individual intent.

Why Visibility Matters

In recent cases (2024–2025), public attention has played a key role in sustaining investigations. Digital platforms preserve evidence, amplify stories, and maintain accountability when media cycles move on.

Accountability

Common reform efforts include independent civilian review boards, body-camera transparency, limits on qualified immunity, and mandatory de-escalation training.

Take Action

This campaign promotes ethical digital activism by encouraging users to share verified information, contact representatives, support community organizations, and advocate for policy-based reform.

Campaign Posts

The campaign includes shareable posts that highlight real cases, explain systemic issues, and encourage sustained civic engagement.
